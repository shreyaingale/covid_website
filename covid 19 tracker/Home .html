Prevention from Corona Virus

stay home, stay safe

how to prevent

how to prevent virus

wash your place

Use alcohol-based disinfectants to clean hard surfaces in your home. Also, clean your phone, laptop, and anything else you use regularly several times a day.

maintain distance

Physical (social) distancing, also means staying home and working remotely when possible. keep a distance of 6 feet (2 m) from other people. You can transmit the virus by speaking to someone in close contact to you.

don't touch face

Avoid touching any part of your face or head, including your mouth, nose, and eyes. Also avoid biting your fingernails. This can give SARS-CoV-2 a chance to go from your hands into your body.

wash your hand

Clean your hands often. Use soap and water, or an alcohol-based hand rub.

use napkin

Cover your mouth while coughing and sneezing . You should also cover your mouth with a tissue or napkin while coughing or sneezing..

wear a mask

Wear a mask in public, especially indoors or when physical distancing is not possible. Properly fitted masks can help prevent the spread of the virus from the person wearing the mask to others

take precautions against covid-19

things you should DO

wash your hand

Regularly and thoroughly clean your hands with either an alcohol-based hand rub or soap and water. This eliminates germs that may be on your hands, including viruses.

always wear a mask

Wear a mask in public, especially indoors or when physical distancing is not possible.Wear a properly fitted mask when physical distancing is not possible and in poorly ventilated settings.

stay home during fever

If you have a fever, cough and difficulty breathing, seek medical attention immediately. Call by telephone first and follow the directions of your local health authority.

things you should NOT DO

avoid close contact with animals

The risk of pets such as dogs or cats spreading the COVID-19 virus to humans is low. However, pets can spread other infections that cause illness, including E. coli and Salmonella, so wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water after interacting with pets.

avoid close contact with peoples

stop shaking hands and hugging peoples. avoid touching other people. Skin-to-skin contact can transmit SARS-CoV-2 from one person to another.

avoid crowded area

Being in a group or gathering makes it more likely that you will be in close contact with someone.Do not gather in group This includes avoiding all religious places of worship, as you may have to sit or stand too close to another congregant. It also includes not congregating at parks or beaches.
